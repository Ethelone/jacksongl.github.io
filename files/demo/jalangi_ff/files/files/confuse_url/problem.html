Confusing URL

At a quick glance, many people might believe the following URL is linking to www.google.com, but if you are using Firefox and click the following link, you will be directed to www.bing.com
http://www.google.com&search_query=ras+bodik+computer+scneince+164&q=test@www.bing.com/search?q=berkeley&qs=n&form=QBLH&pq=berkeley&sc=8-8&sp=-1&sk=&cvid=09f4184817cb4850a70342ae9b3b072d

The syntax of a fully qualified absolute URL is as follows: protocol://login:password@address:port...
The sub-string before the @ character and after the indicator of a hierarchical URL (//) is parsed as credentials to access the resource. Consequently, www.google.com in the link is parsed as the login name rather than the destination. This @-based trick is often used as a technique of online fraud targeted at casual users.
